About Me

Welcome to my small corner of the internet. I am a postdoctoral researcher at the esteemed University of Knowledge, where I spend my days pondering the very fabric of the cosmos and my nights wondering if I remembered to water my plants. My research focuses on the intersection of quantum entanglement and the emergent properties of complex systems. Basically, I try to understand the very, very small to explain the very, very big.

When I'm not wrestling with equations, you can find me exploring ancient hiking trails, attempting to bake the perfect sourdough, or enjoying a particularly zesty cup of coffee.

Current Projects

Research is a team sport. Here are some of the collaborative projects I'm currently involved in.

Project Chimera

A simulation framework designed to model quantum phenomena on classical computing hardware. It's held together by hope, Python scripts, and a lot of coffee.

The Stargazer Initiative

An open-source data analysis pipeline for amateur astronomers. We're making real science accessible to everyone with a telescope and a dream.

Quantum Education Outreach

Developing interactive tools and workshops to explain the weird and wonderful world of quantum mechanics to high school students without making their brains melt.
